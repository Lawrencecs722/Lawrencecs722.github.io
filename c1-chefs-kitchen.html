I Walked Into a Restaurant and Found a Kitchen

My Paradigm Shift from Restaurant Patron to Chef

Sunday, May 3rd, 2015

Based on the video, what's your take on the DBC experience?

This is going to be one challenging course.

In traditional schooling, students get a syllabus, a specific set of resources, daily exercises that need to be validated, periodic testing, all of which is structured by the instructor.  DBC offers analogs to all these things, but there’s a great deal of self-direction involved.

This wasn’t what I was initially expecting, but over the course of my life, self-taught is how I learned some of the more potent skills in my arsenal.  I’m actually looking forward to the paradigm shift.

What is your impression of DBC?

My initial impression of DBC is it’s a crucible.  And like a crucible, DBC provides great intensity and a lot of energy and if used properly could render out a lot of great things. How much useful material comes out of a crucible is largely dependent on the investment, and that will mean all of me.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I’ll be interacting more like an adult in a sharing collaboration, rather than feeling like a child whose hand is being held.  I’ll be comfortable leaning on my experience and the experience of others in addition to traditional resources.

Have your expectations of DBC changed? If so, how?

Yes.  Rather than feeling like I have something to prove (to a teacher), I now feel more like I have something to share.  I’m no longer expecting my instructors to hand-hold me. I will be my own leader, and look forward to learning from the leadership of others.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment?

Absolutely.

Does it make you nervous?

Absolutely.

Talk about what came up for you and anything you want in addition to the questions above.

As I was watching it, I found myself thinking that it’s probably a good thing I didn’t see it beforehand as I wouldn’t have known that I was capable of doing exactly what it said and may never have applied to the school.
